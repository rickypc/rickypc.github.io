Paṭiccasamuppāda​

ye dhammā hetuppabhavā tesaṁ hetuṁ tathāgato āha । tesaṃ ca yo nirodho evaṁvādī mahāsamaṇo॥
7x
Dependent Origination

Dependent origination can be contrasted with the classic Western concept of causation in which an action by one thing is said to cause a change in another thing. Dependent origination instead views the change as being caused by many factors, not just one or even a few.

Those dharmas which arise from a cause, the Tathāgata has declared their cause, and that which is the cessation of them. Thus the great renunciant (sramana) has taught.
