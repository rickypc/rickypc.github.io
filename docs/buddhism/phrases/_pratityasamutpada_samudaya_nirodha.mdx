### Pratītyasamutpāda Samudaya Nirodha

<Phrase {...phrase} {...props} />

<details>
  <summary>
    The [Arising and Cessation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idappaccayat%C4%81) of [Causality (nidāna)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prat%C4%ABtyasamutp%C4%81da#The_twelve_nidanas).
  </summary>
  <div>
    {/* @ts-ignore */}
    > Dependent on ignorance, arises formations.<br />
    > Dependent on formations, arises consciousness.<br />
    > Dependent on consciousness, arises mentality-materiality.<br />
    > Dependent on mentality-materiality, arise the six-sense bases.<br />
    > Dependent on the six-sense bases, arises contact.<br />
    > Dependent on contact, arises feeling.<br />
    > Dependent on feeling, arises craving.<br />
    > Dependent on craving, arises clinging.<br />
    > Dependent on clinging, arises the arranging of kamma.<br />
    > Dependent on the arranging of kamma, arises birth.<br />
    > Dependent on birth, arises aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief and despair.<br />
    > Thus, there is the arising of this whole mass of suffering.

    > Through the entire cessation of ignorance, formations cease.<br />
    > Through the cessation of formations, consciousness ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of consciousness, mentality-materiality ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of mentality-materiality, the six-sense bases cease.<br />
    > Through the cessation of the six-sense bases, contact ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of contact, feeling ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of feeling, craving ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of craving, clinging ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of clinging, the arranging of kamma ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of the arranging of kamma, birth ceases.<br />
    > Through the cessation of birth, aging, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, grief and despair ceases.<br />
    > Thus, there is the cessation of this whole mass of suffering.

  </div>
</details>
