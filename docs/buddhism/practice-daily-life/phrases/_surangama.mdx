  <summary>
    This prayer is traditionally recited to dispel internal and external
    obstacles that hinder spiritual progress. In [East Asian Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asian_Buddhism),
    the [Śūraṅgama Mantra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%9A%C5%ABra%E1%B9%85gama_Mantra)
    (楞嚴咒) is regarded as a powerful dhāraṇī for protection, purification,
    and stabilizing the mind, helping practitioners overcome afflictive
    emotions, harmful influences, and the conditions that obstruct meditation
    and ethical discipline. Like the well-known `oṃ maṇipadme hūṃ` and the
    [Nīlakaṇṭha Dhāraṇī](index.mdx#nīlakaṇṭha-dhāraṇī),
    it is closely associated with the compassionate activity of [Avalokiteśvara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avalokite%C5%9Bvara)
    and is one of the most widely chanted mantras in the [Mahāyāna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahayana)
    world.

    The Śūraṅgama Mantra is also intimately connected with the practice of
    [Sitātapatrā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sitatapatra), the White Parasol
    protector, whose dhāraṇī it parallels in purpose and function. In East
    Asian liturgy it is recited daily, and in Tibetan Buddhism it corresponds
    to the "White Umbrella" ([Wylie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wylie_transliteration): gdugs dkar).
    Its deeper purpose is to subdue the inner "demons" of ignorance, desire,
    and aversion, clearing the path toward clarity and awakening.
  </summary>
