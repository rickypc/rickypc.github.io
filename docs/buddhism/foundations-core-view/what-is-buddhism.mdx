## A Path, Not a Belief System

Buddhism is not built on blind faith, commandments, or a creator God.
It is a **path of understanding** - a way to see reality clearly and reduce
suffering. The Buddha did not ask anyone to believe him; he asked people to
test his teachings through their own experience.

Buddhism is about learning how the mind works, why we suffer, and how we can
free ourselves from the patterns that keep us stuck.

---

## What Buddhism Is Not

Buddhism is often misunderstood. It is **not**:

- a religion that requires worship
- a system of dogma you must accept
- a promise that someone else will save you
- a set of rituals you must perform

It is a practical method for understanding life and transforming the mind.

---

## Why People Study Buddhism

People turn to Buddhism for many reasons:

- To understand why life feels difficult
- To make sense of suffering, loss, and change
- To find clarity in a confusing world
- To develop compassion and wisdom
- To break old habits and create a better future
- To understand karma and rebirth
- To seek liberation from the cycle of suffering

Buddhism offers answers to deep questions:

- Why am I here?
- Why do I experience certain challenges?
- Why do I repeat the same patterns?
- How can I live with more peace and purpose?

It provides a framework for understanding life as it truly is, not as we wish
it to be.

This collection of pages is designed to help anyone - including my future
self - understand [Buddhism](/docs/category/buddhism) clearly and quickly,
without confusion or unnecessary complexity.

You can read it in order or jump to any section. A simple way to navigate:

- **[Foundations & Core View](/docs/category/foundations-core-view)**

  Learn the Buddhist worldview - why we are born, what life is for, and how
  karma shapes our experiences - while exploring core teachings such as
  dependent origination, karma, suffering, and liberation to understand how
  these principles reveal the nature of reality.

- **[Practice & Daily Life](/docs/category/practice-daily-life)**

  Build a steady foundation through meditation, mindfulness, and ethical daily
  habits that support clarity, compassion, and emotional balance, while
  applying Buddhist wisdom to emotions, relationships, decisions, death, and
  rebirth so the teachings become a lived, practical part of everyday life.

- **[Teachers, Lineage & Modern Practice](/docs/category/teachers-lineage-modern-practice)**

  Understand how to relate to teachers, lineage, permissions, and retreat
  culture with confidence. Learn what lineage truly means, how to form healthy
  relationships with teachers, and how to navigate transmissions and
  monetization in modern settings.

- **[Rituals & Ceremonies](/docs/category/rituals-ceremonies)**

  Discover the purpose behind mantras, prayers, offerings, and consecrations,
  and how these practices help purify the mind and deepen intention.

This set is meant to be **simple, direct, and timeless** - a guide you can
return to again and again as your understanding deepens.
