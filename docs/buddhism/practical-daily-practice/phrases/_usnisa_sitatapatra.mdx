### Uṣṇīṣa Sitātapatrā Hṛdaya

<Phrase {...phrase} {...props} />

<details>
  <summary>
    [Sitātapatrā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sitatapatra), the "White
    Parasol", is a powerful protector against supernatural danger in [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism),
    revered in both the [Mahāyāna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahayana) and
    [Vajrayāna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrayana) traditions. She is
    believed to emanate from the [Uṣṇīṣa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ushnisha)
    of [Gautama Buddha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gautama_Buddha) while he
    was abiding in deep samādhi, manifesting as an independent deity whose
    mantrapada offers protection from calamities, malevolent forces, and
    harmful influences. Brilliant white and radiating compassion, she is often
    depicted with a white parasol and, in her most elaborate form, with a
    thousand heads, arms, and eyes that watch over and safeguard beings.

    Reciting her dhāraṇī is traditionally said to avert obstacles, purify
    defilements, and ensure a favorable rebirth, including the aspiration to be
    reborn in [Amitābha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amit%C4%81bha)'s
    [pure land](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pure_land) of [Sukhāvatī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukhavati).
    Sitātapatrā's practice is widely used for protection, healing, and
    establishing protective boundaries, making her one of the most important
    guardians in Vajrayāna Buddhism.
  </summary>
