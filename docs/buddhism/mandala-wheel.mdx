## Supplies

To prepare the Maṇḍala Wheel, you'll need the following:

- Maṇḍala wheels strip <Link href="/pdf/mandala-wheels-strip.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open maṇḍala wheels strip"><GrPrint /></Link>
(printed [double-sided](https://helpx.adobe.com/acrobat/kb/print-double-sided-acrobat-reader.html#print-double-sided),
flip on the short edge) or maṇḍala wheels <Link href="/pdf/mandala-wheels.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open maṇḍala wheels"><GrPrint /></Link>
  - [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel
  - [Vasudhārā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasudhara) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel
  - [Ganapati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesha) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel
  - [Aṣṭamaṅgala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashtamangala) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel
  - [Viśvavajra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajra) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel
- [Honey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honey)
- [Butter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Butter)
- [Sugar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugar)
- [Gemstone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemstone) powders, such as:
  - [Diamond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamond_(gemstone))
  - Green [turquoise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turquoise)
  - [Red coral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precious_coral)
  - [Ruby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruby)
  - [Mother of pearl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nacre)
  - [Carnelian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnelian)

  - [Obsidian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian)
- Red and white [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood)
- Nice cotton cloth or silk in [<span style={{ color: '#ff9933' }}>saffron</span>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron_(color)) or deity-specific colors

## Instructions

1. Prepare the sweet paste offering

    Mix honey, butter, and sugar together. Combine this with [gemstone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemstone) powders
    and red and white [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood) to create a fragrant, sacred paste.

2. Join the [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala) and [Vasudhārā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasudhara) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheels

    Use the sweet paste to stick the [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala) and [Vasudhārā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasudhara) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheels
    together - symbolically as if they are embracing.

    - [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala) should be placed on top, facing downward.

3. Mark the outer surface

    After joining them, mark the outside of the [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheels to identify:
    - Which one is [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala)
    - The direction of the heads of the deities

    This helps ensure correct orientation when placing them inside the statue
    or stupa. The heads of the deities should face the back of
    the statue/stupa - so if they were "standing up", they'd be facing
    the same direction as the statue/stupa.

4. Layer the remaining maṇḍala wheels

    Beneath the [Vasudhārā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasudhara) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel, add the following in order:
    - [Ganapati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesha) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel (facing up)
    - [Aṣṭamaṅgala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashtamangala) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel (facing up)
    - [Viśvavajra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajra) [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheel (facing down, toward the base - like [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala))

    Use the sweet paste offering to stick each layer together.

5. Wrap the entire set

    Once assembled, wrap all the [maṇḍala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala) wheels together
    with a nice cloth - preferably cotton or silk in [<span style={{ color: '#ff9933' }}>saffron</span>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron_(color))
    or deity-specific colors.
