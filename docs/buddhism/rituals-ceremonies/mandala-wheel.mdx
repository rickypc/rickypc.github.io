## Supplies

To prepare the Maṇḍala Wheel, you'll need the following:

- Maṇḍala wheels strip <Link href="/pdf/mandala-wheels-strip.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open maṇḍala wheels strip"><GrPrint /></Link>
  (printed [double-sided](https://helpx.adobe.com/acrobat/kb/print-double-sided-acrobat-reader.html#print-double-sided),
  flip on the short edge) or maṇḍala wheels <Link href="/pdf/mandala-wheels.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open maṇḍala wheels"><GrPrint /></Link>

  - [Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala) maṇḍala wheel
  - [Vasudhārā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasudhara) maṇḍala wheel
  - [Ganapati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesha) maṇḍala wheel
  - [Aṣṭamaṅgala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashtamangala) maṇḍala wheel
  - [Viśvavajra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajra) maṇḍala wheel

- [Honey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honey)
- [Butter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Butter)
- [Sugar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugar)
- [Gemstone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemstone) powders, such as:
  - [Diamond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diamond_(gemstone))
  - Green [turquoise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turquoise)

  [<span style={{ color: '#ff9933' }}>saffron</span>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron_(color))

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the sweet paste offering

Mix honey, butter, and sugar together. Combine this with [gemstone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemstone)
powders and red and white [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood)
to create a fragrant, sacred paste.

### 2. Join the Jambhala and Vasudhārā maṇḍala wheels

Use the sweet paste to stick the
[Jambhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambhala) and
[Vasudhārā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasudhara)
maṇḍala wheels together - symbolically as if they are embracing.

- Jambhala should be placed on top, **facing downward**.

### 3. Mark the outer surface

After joining them, mark the outside of the maṇḍala wheels to identify:

- Which one is **Jambhala**
- The direction of the **heads of the deities**

This ensures correct orientation when placing them inside a statue or stupa.
The heads of the deities should face the **back** of the statue/stupa - so if
they were "standing up", they would face the same direction as the statue.

### 4. Layer the remaining maṇḍala wheels

Beneath the Vasudhārā wheel, add the following in order:

1. **Ganapati** maṇḍala wheel - facing **up**
2. **Aṣṭamaṅgala** maṇḍala wheel - facing **up**
3. **Viśvavajra** maṇḍala wheel - facing **down**, toward the base
   (like Jambhala)

Use the sweet paste offering to stick each layer together.

### 5. Wrap the entire set

Once assembled, wrap all the maṇḍala wheels together with a nice cloth -
preferably cotton or silk in
[<span style={{ color: '#ff9933' }}>saffron</span>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron_(color))
or deity-specific colors.

This completes the preparation of the Maṇḍala Wheel.
