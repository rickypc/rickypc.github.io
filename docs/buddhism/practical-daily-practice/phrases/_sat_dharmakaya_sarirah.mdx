### Ṣáṭ Dharmakāya Śarīrāḥ

<Phrase {...phrase} {...props} />

<details>
  <summary>
    The Six [Dharmakāya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharmak%C4%81ya)
    [Relics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%9Aar%C4%ABra#Terminology)[^1]
    are a collection of five great [dhāraṇīs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharani)[^2]
    together with Kriyayogatantra - Immaculate Radiance[^3]. As the most
    important relics of the [dharmakāya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharmak%C4%81ya)[^4],
    they are traditionally placed inside Buddha statues, stupas, and sacred
    sculptures.

    The five great [dhāraṇīs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharani)[^2] are:
    <ol>
      <li>Uṣṇīṣavijayā[^5]</li>
      <li>Vimala Uṣṇīṣa[^6]</li>
      <li>Guhyadhātukaraṇḍa[^7]</li>
      <li>Bodhigarbhālaṃkāralakṣa[^8]</li>
      <li>Pratītyasamutpādahṛdaya[^9]</li>
    </ol>

    Some traditions present the [Dharmakāya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharmak%C4%81ya)
    [relics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%9Aar%C4%ABra#Terminology) as a
    set of four. This grouping includes:
    <ol>
      <li>Vimala Uṣṇīṣa[^6]</li>
      <li>Guhyadhātukaraṇḍa[^7]</li>
      <li>Kriyayogatantra - Immaculate Radiance[^3]</li>
      <li>Bodhigarbhālaṃkāralakṣa[^8]</li>
    </ol>

    A Buddha statue or stupa that has been consecrated with these relics is
    regarded not merely as a crafted object but as a sacred presence that can
    offer blessings and restore positive conditions for anyone who sponsors it,
    sees it, touches it, circumambulates it, or even encounters the wind that
    passes by it.
  </summary>
