### Mahā Cundī Durgā Dhāranī

<Phrase {...phrase} {...props} />

<details>
  <summary>
    [Cundī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cundi_(Buddhism)) is a female Indian
    [Buddhist deity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhist_deities) which remains
    popular in [East Asian Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asian_Buddhism).
    In [Chinese Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism),
    she is associated with the practice of the well known Cundī [dhāraṇī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharani),
    which is performed along with a specific [mudra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mudra) (hand gesture),
    as well as the use of a circular mirror. She is considered to be able to
    purify negative karma, provide protection, support spiritual practice which
    allows one to quickly attain Buddhahood.
