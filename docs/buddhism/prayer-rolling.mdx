## Supplies

- Prayer strips on [<span style={{ color: '#f4c430' }}>saffron</span>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron_(color))
  color paper (e.g. Āryatārā prayer strips:
  <Link href="/pdf/arya-tara.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open Āryatārā prayer roll">Buddha statue/stupa</Link>,
  <Link href="/pdf/arya-tara-wheel.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open Āryatārā prayer wheel">prayer wheel</Link>; or
  <Link href="/pdf/cintamani-dharmacakra-yasti.pdf" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" title="Open Cintāmaṇi Dharmacakra Yaṣṭi prayer strip">prayer wheel life force pillar</Link>).
- A pure incense stick (coreless).
- Thin cotton cloth or [lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lama)'s robe ([<span style={{ color: '#ff9933' }}>saffron</span>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saffron_(color)) or deity specific color).
- A paper glue stick.
- Eco-friendly <span style={{ color: 'red' }}>red</span> dye or marker.
- A scissors.
- An oblong block of wood.

## Instructions

### For Buddha Statue/[Stupa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stupa)

The video shows [how to roll prayer strips](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PwSm7r5ppww).
More [videos](https://bodhipath.org/centers/natural-bridge-stupa-mantra-rolling/) with wider prayer strips.
The [essential prayers for holy objects](https://fpmt.org/wp-content/uploads/teachers/zopa/advice/essential-mantras-for-holy-objects/essentialmantrasholyobjects_c5.pdf) will explain further on the topic.

- Cut off a piece of pure incense stick to the width of the prayer strip.
- Roll the first sheet with the pure incense stick glued inside, beginning at
  the "head" (start) of the text, which faces inwards.
- Hold the top and bottom of the pure incense stick to roll it easier.
- Additional strips are then added with the same orientation, in groups of one or more -
  depending upon our dexterity, with an overlap of about 2 [cm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centimetre)
  towards the end of the previous strips.
- Tighten the prayer roll by rolling them on a firm surface with an oblong block of wood.
- Finish the roll with a single strip, gluing firmly the final 2 [cm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centimetre).
- Mark the top of the prayer roll with red dye or marker.
- Wrap the outside of the prayer roll with cloth to the exact width of the prayer roll.
  Glue the cloth all around and over the junction of its ends.
- Write the prayer name and the repetition numbers on a small piece of paper
  and glue that outside of the prayer roll.
