### Śaraṇagamana Cittotpāda Gāthā

<Phrase {...phrase} {...props} />

<details>
  <summary>
    A verse for taking [refuge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refuge_in_Buddhism)
    and giving rise to [bodhicitta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodhicitta).
    It expresses the practitioner's commitment to rely on the [Three Jewels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Jewels_and_Three_Roots)
    and to cultivate the altruistic intention to attain awakening for the
    benefit of all beings. Recited at the beginning of [Mahāyāna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahayana)
    and [Vajrayāna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrayana) practices, it
    establishes the foundation of refuge and compassion that supports all
    subsequent meditation and activity.
  </summary>
