### Mahāpratisarā Vidyā Dhāraṇī

<Phrase {...phrase} {...props} />

<details>
  <summary>
    The **Mahāpratisarā Vidyā Dhāraṇī** is one of the most revered protective
    formulas in the Buddhist world, centered on the goddess [Mahāpratisarā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mah%C4%81pratisar%C4%81),
    a radiant embodiment of safeguard, healing, and karmic purification.
    Regarded as the "[Great Amulet](https://www.rigpawiki.org/index.php?title=The_Great_Amulet)",
    this dhāraṇī occupies a central place within the [Pañcarakṣā](https://www.rigpawiki.org/index.php?title=Pancha_Raksha)
    (Five Great Protectors), a collection of scriptures believed to shield
    practitioners from illness, misfortune, and untimely death.

    Often depicted as a **Vidyārājñī** ("Queen of Mantras"), Mahāpratisarā is
    invoked for her power to dispel obstacles, neutralize malevolent forces,
    and grant well-being to those who recite, write, or carry her dhāraṇī. The
    mantra itself contains potent syllables -such as *Oṃ*, *Hūṃ*, and *Phaṭ*-
    associated with illumination, protection, and the cutting of negativity.
    Across South Asia, Tibet, and East Asia, this dhāraṇī has been inscribed on
    amulets, printed on talismans, and recited in rituals for centuries,
    earning a reputation as one of Buddhism's most effective and compassionate
    protective formulas.
  </summary>
